I’m Tingyin Wang, a Ph.D. student developing reliable, privacy-aware machine learning methods for complex and distributed data.

Explore my work ↓

I build statistical tools that learn across populations—without losing sight of privacy, robustness, or real-world impact.

My work sits at the intersection of statistical learning and machine learning, with an emphasis on methods that remain dependable when data are heterogeneous, high-dimensional, or distributed across institutions. I am advised by Prof. Yong Chen at Penn.

Research

Federated & Transfer Learning

Learning across heterogeneous data sources while preserving privacy and extracting shared signal.

Reliable Machine Learning

Robust, causal, and high-dimensional methods with an eye toward reliability and performance monitoring.

Healthcare AI & RWE

Statistical methods for real-world evidence, target trial emulation, biomarker discovery, and clinical data.

Functional Data Analysis

Clustering and modeling complex objects, including spatial, temporal, and shape-valued observations.
